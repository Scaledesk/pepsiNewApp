Treasury Scope

As Sales is the face of the company for Customers, R&D for innovation, Reporting for Sector, Treasury is the face of the company for Banks. Treasury is the part of all activities where Banks act as key Stakeholders. Treasury in a company is key in determining the firm’s financial strategy and financial policy – advising on what businesses to invest in, organizing the appropriate funding for this, and controlling the risk in the organization.

India Treasury Team - Organization Structure

Treasury Department in PepsiCo India Region is a part of Finance Control Team stationed at the PepsiCo India headquarters – Gurgaon. The team looks after all treasury related <p/> operations in India Region - India, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka and Nepal.

The team is  lead by India Treasurer. Treasury Manager reports to India Treasurer while Treasury Consultant (Operations) and Treasury Consultant (Exports and Insurance) report to Treasury manager.

Cash Flow Operations India Region

Treasury facilitates:

  • Payments

Treasury forms the final leg of all kinds of Payments.

Treasury does the following

  1. Decides which bank to be co-ordinated for Payments depending on Criteria like – Relationship, Charges, IT Infrastructure, Processing Time, etc.
  2. Ensures local/foreign payments are as per RBI norms
  3. Ensures payment approvals are as per Authority Matrix & Board Resolution.
  4. Ensure smooth processing of advances & urgent payments
  5. Ensures funds are available for smooth transactions.
  6. Ensures smooth cross-country payments (buying/selling functional currencies & paying off)

Treasury in association with other finance verticals, have outsourced the payments. Today, IBM’s  P2P team takes care of invoice processing (normal/advances/urgent), discrepancy reporting, reconciliation, and even posting of entries in our books of accounts.

Treasury should be approached in one of the following cases.

  1. Queries in regards to rightful approver for any payment
  2. New/Exceptional/unique payments which requires a feasibility check as per RBI guidelines
  3. Queries in regards to pre-requisites or documents required for successful completion of transactions
  4. Queries on urgent payments, for example, on-time payment to government bodies

Another Critical area of Cash flow Operations is Collections

Treasury team co-ordinates from the flow of money from Distributors or Modern Trade Partners & On-premise outlets to Bank.

Treasury does the following:

  1. Maps collections accounts to various distributors as on when created.
  2. Maps collections to respective GLs for clarity in recording in the books of accounts.
  3. Intimates about Bank holidays and other days when collections can be disrupted.

Treasury should be approached in one of the following cases

  1. When units require clarity – If PepsiCo has received funds against a particular claim (Kindly note, Treasury has provided access to units and IBM with the viewing rights in Banks Portal)
  2. Programs like Channel Financing and NACH as a product </p> their details and implementation.


Cash Management

In Cash Management, Treasury is responsible for the cash line in the Balance Sheet.

The team does in the following:

  1. Forecast on Payment and collection, </p> both Short term and long term
  2. Ensuring availability of funds for thousands of payments on a daily basis.
  3. Monitoring Debt Position by timely and correct fund transfers. This is to ensure maximum interest saving.
  4. Monitoring Cash Position by investing surplus cash
  5. Buying/Selling of non-functional currency at a reasonable rate
  6. Circulating Bank Statements to concerned stakeholders for internal reconciliation with the books of accounts.

Trade – Imports & Exports

  1. Imports

PepsiCo makes huge imports payments for

  1. Raw Materials (e.g. Oats) /Packaging Material(e.g. pre-forms)
  2. Finished goods (e.g. Doritos)
  3. Capex related (e.g. plant & machineries).

Treasury should be contacted if any import/exports which may require RBI permissions or bound by Banking limitations.

  1. Exports

PepsiCo exports

  1. Raw Materials/Packaging Materials (e.g. spice mix & concentrate)
  2. Finished goods (e.g. lays, kurkure & Quaker) to countries like Bangladesh, Nepal or Sri Lanka.

Treasury co-ordinates and ensures all documents are in places for Banking compliance point of view.

Treasury handles the crucial task of providing documents to banks

Escalation to Treasury should happen if

(i) PepsiCo needs to Export to countries other than the existing/regular ones or

(ii) Issues in Existing Exports process

Forex & Hedging

  • Forex

The imports payments are in non-local currencies like USD, AUD or EURO.

Treasury  plays a pivotal role:

  1. To Buy or Sell non-local currency at reasonable rates.
  2. To reduce currency risk and minimize Forex Loss
  3. To Ensure all documents are in place for internal audit.
  4. To Correctly represent of all such transaction in books of account.


For Example

Import payments & Export collections are in USD, AUD, EURO, JPY , BDT . Apart from import and exports, there are inter country cross-charges in foreign currency. In such scenario, PepsiCo is exposed to currency risk or transactional risk.


  • Hedging

Hedging is analogous to insuring. Hedging ensures guaranteed inflow/outflow of cash at a particular rate, provides a clearer picture about our investments and/or payments. In this VUCA world, PepsiCo takes calculative decision on where to hedge. Hedging in India is largely governed by the corporate FX team.


Example – Here is an example where Hedging addresses the exposure of a futuristic payment.

Assume, PepsiCo INC have provided a loan of USD 20MM to PepsiCo India to facilitate expansion of business in India. PepsiCo India promises to repay the same on some later date (say 3 years from now). India business converts this foreign currency (USD) to local currency (INR) and  uses the same to expand the business.

One can clearly observe that 3 years is enough to have huge uncertainty on the exchange rate. One learning is INR has devalued from INR 45/USD in 2011-12 to INR 65/USD in 2014-15. The dollar repayment to PepsiCo INC is hence hedged as per the repayment schedule. Hedging cost is generally around 8%. The team also works closely with bank to ensure all future cross-currency transactions are rightly hedged.

Note – Treasury assesses the exposure (currency risk in the above example) before going ahead with the hedging.

For more detail, you may refer to the course on Managing Foreign Exchange in MyLearnings.

Investment in Provident Fund – Planning, maintaining & Executing

  • PF Investments

PepsiCo unlike other major corporates has its own PF Trust. The process moves from

  • Identifying the investable surplus in any month.
  • Co-ordination with Darashaw (PF Investment advisory Vendor) for identifying areas of Investments.
  • Analysing credibility of proposed investments
  • In association of PF Trust Committee (considering the limits of type of investments), takes decisions on where to invest.
  • Ensures timely documentation and approvals
  • Co-ordinates with banks for movement of funds and closure of Investments
  • In association with Megasoftsol, keeps record of Investments
  • Ensures timely maturity of Bonds
  • Ensures investments are within the categorical limits (Government Bonds, Corporate Bonds, T Bills, Mutual Funds, Fix Deposits) sets by Ministry of Labour

Treasury reports the investments to the Ministry on a Yearly basis. The investments may be of any tenor – as small as 7 days to as large as 30 yrs.
